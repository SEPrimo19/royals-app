Plain-Language Summary

This document tells you, in plain words, exactly what the Royals app does with your data and how we expect Royals to treat each other on the platform.

The short version:

  • Your journal entries are encrypted on your phone. Nobody but you can read them — not even the developers.
  • Your anonymous prayers stay anonymous to other members. Pastoral leadership can see them only for safeguarding (in case of crisis).
  • Your private messages with your cell leader are visible only to you and them.
  • We collect only the data we need to run the app and meet Compassion compliance requirements.
  • You can delete your account at any time, and your data is removed.
  • If you are under 18, please ask a parent or guardian to read this with you and give consent before installing.

If you have any questions, message Jhon Clarence Barbarona Rulona before signing up.

5. Anonymous Prayer — The Details

When you post a prayer request with the "Anonymous" toggle ON:

  • The Prayer Wall shows your post with the name "A Royal in Prayer" and a dove icon instead of your photo
  • Other members and your cell leader cannot see your identity
  • The "Pray for this" count is still tracked publicly, but who tapped it stays private
  • Pastoral leadership retains visibility on the identity behind anonymous posts. This is a deliberate safeguarding measure, not a peeping power: if a prayer indicates someone is in crisis (depression, abuse, self-harm risk), pastoral leadership can quietly reach out to that member directly. They do not publicly expose who posted what.

If full anonymity (even from pastoral staff) is critical to you, please share that prayer request offline with someone you trust instead of posting it in the app.

6. Your Journal — The Details

Your devotional journal is the most private feature in Royals:

  • Every journal entry is encrypted on your device with AES-256-GCM before it ever leaves your phone
  • The encryption key is derived from a key stored in your phone's Android Keystore (the secure hardware-backed vault Android uses for things like fingerprint data)
  • Even when your encrypted journal entry is stored in our cloud database, no one — not the developers, not the pastors, not anyone — can read it without your device
  • If you uninstall the app and reinstall it on the same phone, your journal entries remain readable
  • If you switch phones, journal entries from your old phone become unreadable (the encryption key never leaves the original device)

This is the strongest privacy guarantee in the app, by design. Your journal is between you and God.

7. Compassion Sponsored Youth — Special Notes

If you are a Compassion International sponsored youth and you opt to enter your PH867-XXXX ID:

  • Your Compassion ID is stored in your profile and used to label your compliance reports
  • Your attendance, devotional, and meditation activity are automatically tallied for monthly compliance
  • You can generate your own monthly compliance PDF at any time from the My Progress screen
  • Your cell leader and pastoral leadership can also generate this report for you (for verification)
  • The PDF report can be shared with your Compassion worker via Messenger, email, or printed copy — you choose

We do not transmit your Compassion data directly to Compassion International. The compliance PDF is yours to share manually. We do not share it with anyone outside The Kingdom Builders without your explicit consent.

8. How Data Is Stored and Protected

  • Hosting: Royals data is hosted on Supabase (a managed PostgreSQL service). Supabase is SOC 2 Type 2 certified and applies industry-standard encryption at rest and in transit.
  • Database security: Every table in the database has row-level security (RLS) policies. These policies enforce, at the database layer, that members can only read data they are authorized to see. The app cannot bypass these rules.
  • Transport: All communication between your phone and our servers uses HTTPS (TLS 1.2 or higher).
  • Journal encryption: AES-256-GCM, with the key bound to your phone's Android Keystore (see Section 6).
  • Refresh tokens: Stored in EncryptedSharedPreferences on your device, not in plain DataStore.
  • Source code: Royals is open source. You can review the code at github.com/SEPrimo19/royals-app to verify these claims.

10. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Your data is retained while your account is active
  • You can delete your account at any time: Settings -> Privacy -> Delete My Account
  • Account deletion removes: your profile, your journal entries, your private messages, your reaction history, and your account record
  • It does NOT remove: prayer requests you posted (these remain on the Prayer Wall so others can keep praying, but they are anonymized to "A Royal in Prayer" so your identity is no longer linked); Life Feed posts (these remain visible but with your name removed); attendance records that have already been counted toward an aggregated monthly Compassion report (we keep aggregate counts but disconnect them from your identity)
  • If you want even those traces removed, contact Jhon Clarence Barbarona Rulona directly and we will manually purge as much as is reasonably possible
  • Encrypted backups of the database may retain your data for up to 30 days before being overwritten

11. Your Rights Under the Philippine Data Privacy Act

As a data subject under RA 10173, you have the right to:

  • Be informed — you are reading this notice now, fulfilling this right
  • Access your data — request a copy of everything we hold on you
  • Correct inaccurate data — via the app's Edit Profile screen or by contacting us
  • Object to processing — ask us to stop using your data for any specific purpose
  • Delete or block your data — via the in-app account deletion or by contacting us
  • Data portability — ask for your data in a structured, commonly-used format (we will provide JSON within 30 days of request)
  • File a complaint — with the National Privacy Commission (privacy.gov.ph) if you believe we have mishandled your data

To exercise any of these rights, message Jhon Clarence Barbarona Rulona or email the contact listed in Section 14.

12. Parental Consent for Minors (Under 18)

Royals is intended for youth ministry members ages 13 to 30. If you are under 18 years old:

  • By installing and signing up, you are confirming that a parent or guardian is aware of the app and has given consent
  • Parents/guardians may request to review their child's data, restrict what the child can post, or request account deletion at any time by contacting us
  • If you are aware of a minor using the app without parental consent, please let us know and we will follow up

Part 2: Community Guidelines

Royals is a digital extension of our youth ministry. The way we treat each other in the app should reflect the same values we live out in person.

1. How We Treat Each Other

  • Be kind. Speak to others the way you would want to be spoken to. Encourage, lift up, and pray for each other.
  • Be honest. Share authentically. The Life Feed and Prayer Wall work best when we are real with each other.
  • Be humble. Disagree with grace. We are a body, not a battleground.
  • Respect anonymous posts. If you know who posted an anonymous prayer (or strongly suspect), do not call them out publicly or privately. Honor their choice to remain unnamed.

2. What Is Not Allowed

The following will result in content removal and may lead to account suspension:

  • Bullying, harassment, mockery, or personal attacks toward any member
  • Hate speech of any kind — including discriminatory language about race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, or disability
  • Sexually explicit, suggestive, or inappropriate content
  • Violent or graphic content
  • Sharing illegal content, including pirated material
  • Promoting drugs, alcohol abuse, gambling, or other harmful activities
  • Doxxing — revealing the real identity of someone who posted anonymously
  • Impersonating another member, leader, or pastor
  • Spam, commercial promotion, MLM recruitment, or off-topic advertising
  • Using the app to coordinate activity outside our ministry's values
  • Any content that violates Philippine law

3. Anonymous Posts Are Sacred

When someone posts a prayer anonymously, they are trusting the community with something they could not say openly. Treat that trust as sacred:

  • Do not try to guess or expose who posted
  • Do not screenshot anonymous posts to share elsewhere
  • Do not message someone privately saying "I know that was you"
  • Just pray. That is what they asked for.

4. Content That Gets Removed

Cell leaders, youth officers, and pastors have the authority to remove content that violates these guidelines. When content is removed:

  • The author is notified privately with the reason
  • First-time violations are usually a warning
  • Repeated or severe violations may result in temporary or permanent account suspension

5. Reporting a Problem

If you see something that violates these guidelines:

  • Use the Flag button on any prayer or post (available on every item)
  • Or message your cell leader directly
  • Or message Jhon Clarence Barbarona Rulona for serious matters

Reports are reviewed privately. The person who reported is never revealed to the person being reported.

6. If You Are in Crisis

If you are experiencing depression, thoughts of self-harm, abuse, or any crisis situation, please reach out beyond the app:

  • Your cell leader, youth officer, or pastor — they want to help
  • National Center for Mental Health crisis hotline: 1553 (toll-free, 24/7, nationwide)
  • HOPELINE PH: (02) 8804-4673 or 0917-558-4673
  • If you are in immediate physical danger, call 911

The app can help connect you with people who care, but trained help is sometimes what you need most. We honor you for reaching out.

7. Account Suspension and Restoration

  • If your account is suspended, you will be notified with the reason and the duration
  • You can appeal a suspension by contacting Jhon Clarence Barbarona Rulona
  • Permanent removal is reserved for severe or repeated violations, and is a last resort after pastoral conversation

8. These Guidelines May Be Updated

As our ministry grows and learns, these guidelines may evolve. Updates will be posted in the app and announced via push notification.
